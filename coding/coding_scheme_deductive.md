# Goffmanian Coding Scheme for GenAI Opacity Mechanisms
## Inter-Rater Coding Protocol v1.0

---

## OVERVIEW

This document provides a systematic coding scheme for analyzing interview transcripts through the lens of Goffman's dramaturgical framework, specifically operationalized for the study of GenAI opacity mechanisms in workplace settings. The scheme is designed to enable reliable inter-rater coding across three coders.

### Theoretical Foundation

Our coding scheme draws on three core Goffmanian concepts:

**1. Footing (Goffman, 1981)**
The alignment speakers take toward their utterances, distinguishing among:
- *Animator*: The person who physically produces the utterance
- *Author*: The person who composes the words
- *Principal*: The person whose position is established by the words

**2. Impression Management (Goffman, 1959)**
The techniques by which individuals control the impressions others form:
- *Front stage*: Performances for audiences
- *Back stage*: Regions hidden from audiences
- *Audience segregation*: Maintaining distinct performances for different audiences

**3. Stigma Management (Goffman, 1963)**
Strategies for managing "spoiled identity":
- *Passing*: Concealing discreditable attributes
- *Covering*: Minimizing the obtrusiveness of known attributes
- *Disclosure*: Strategic revelation of hidden attributes

### Unit of Analysis

The primary unit of analysis is the **utterance cluster**: a coherent sequence of participant statements addressing a single topic or concern. Utterance clusters may span multiple conversational turns but should represent a thematically unified segment.

Code each utterance cluster for *all applicable* codes. Multiple codes from the same category and across categories may apply to a single cluster.

### Coding Procedure

1. Read the full transcript to understand context
2. Identify utterance clusters relevant to AI use
3. For each cluster, apply all applicable codes from the mechanism and interactional categories
4. Record verbatim quote, code(s), and brief analytic memo
5. Flag ambiguous cases for team discussion

## PART 1: OPACITY MECHANISM CODES

Each opacity mechanism is coded through multiple indicators. Apply the **parent code** (e.g., VOICE) whenever *any* child code applies.

---

### VOICE: Voice Opacity Codes

**Definition**: Discourse indicating concern about AI filtering stance cues, personal voice, or authorial identity.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| VOICE_POSSESS | **Possessive voice/style claims.** Participant asserts ownership of voice, style, or personality. Look for: "my voice," "my style," "my personality," "my tone," "my way of writing/speaking." |
| VOICE_SOUND | **"Sounds like me" discourse.** Explicit concern about whether output sounds authentic to the participant. Look for: "sounds like me," "doesn't sound like me," "sounds natural," "sounds authentic." |
| VOICE_GENERIC | **Generic/robotic concern.** Output characterized as impersonal, generic, or machine-like. Look for: "too generic," "robotic," "artificial," "impersonal," "bland," "cookie-cutter." |
| VOICE_LOSE | **Voice loss anxiety.** Fear of losing personal voice through AI use. Look for: "lose my voice," "losing my style," "voice getting lost," "style disappearing." |
| VOICE_PRESERVE | **Voice preservation effort.** Active effort to maintain voice despite AI use. Look for: "preserve my voice," "keep my style," "maintain my personality," "protect my voice." |
| VOICE_EDIT | **Editing to personalize.** Modifying AI output to restore personal markers. Look for: "edit to sound like me," "rewrite in my voice," "tweak to my style," "personalize the output." |
| VOICE_TOUCH | **"Personal/human touch" discourse.** Valuing human elements AI cannot provide. Look for: "personal touch," "human touch," "human element," "warmth," "personality." |
| VOICE_AUTH | **Authenticity concern.** Explicit authenticity framing. Look for: "authentic," "genuine," "real," "true to myself." |

**Boundary condition**: Do NOT code routine editing without personalization intent (e.g., "I edit for grammar").

---

### VULN: Vulnerability Opacity Codes

**Definition**: Discourse indicating use of AI to mask uncertainty, struggle, or knowledge gaps; creation of expertise facades.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| VULN_STUCK | **"Stuck, then AI" pattern.** Turning to AI when blocked or confused. Look for: "when I'm stuck," "when I'm lost," "when I don't know," "when I'm struggling," "when I hit a wall." |
| VULN_GAP | **"Fill gaps" language.** Using AI to cover knowledge deficiencies. Look for: "fill the gap," "cover my weakness," "bridge the knowledge gap," "make up for what I don't know." |
| VULN_EXPERT | **Expertise facade.** Appearing more expert than one is. Look for: "look like I know," "appear competent," "seem knowledgeable," "fake expertise," "pretend I understand." |
| VULN_OUTSIDE | **"Outside expertise" marker.** Explicit acknowledgment of operating beyond competence. Look for: "outside my expertise," "not my area," "not my specialty," "beyond my knowledge." |
| VULN_QUICK | **"Quick expertise" acquisition.** AI enabling rapid knowledge without learning. Look for: "quickly understand," "instant expertise," "fast answer," "get up to speed." |
| VULN_HIDE | **Hiding struggle.** Concealing difficulty or confusion from others. Look for: "hide my struggle," "don't show I'm confused," "conceal my difficulty," "mask my uncertainty." |

**Boundary condition**: Code only when AI use is linked to managing perception of competence. Routine learning assistance without competence-performance framing should NOT be coded.

---

### PROV: Provenance Opacity Codes

**Definition**: Discourse indicating accountability gaps, inability to explain or defend AI outputs, disclosure dilemmas, and trust/verification concerns.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| PROV_VERIFY | **Verification practice.** Checking AI output for accuracy. Look for: "verify," "check," "validate," "fact-check," "double-check," "confirm." |
| PROV_HALLUC | **Hallucination concern.** Awareness of AI fabrication risk. Look for: "hallucinate," "make things up," "fabricate," "invent," "incorrect," "inaccurate," "false." |
| PROV_TRUST | **Trust/reliability discourse.** Concerns about trusting AI output. Look for: "trust the output," "rely on," "reliable," "can I trust," "how much to trust." |
| PROV_DISCL | **Disclosure of AI use.** Discussion of telling others about AI use. Look for: "tell them I used AI," "mention AI," "disclose AI," "admit AI use," "share that I used." |
| PROV_HIDE | **Hiding AI use.** Concealing AI assistance from others. Look for: "don't tell," "don't mention," "keep secret," "hide AI use," "private," "secretly." |
| PROV_STIGMA | **Stigma discourse.** Explicit reference to stigma around AI use. Look for: "stigma," "judged," "frowned upon," "look down on," "negative perception." |
| PROV_DEFEND | **Defend/explain concern.** Worry about ability to defend AI-assisted work. Look for: "explain why," "defend my work," "justify," "accountable," "responsible for." |
| PROV_SOURCE | **Source attribution.** Concerns about tracing information origins. Look for: "where did this come from," "source," "citation," "reference," "provenance." |

**Boundary condition**: PROV_VERIFY is common and should be coded even for routine checking. Other PROV codes require more explicit engagement with accountability/disclosure.

---

### ATTN: Attention Opacity Codes

**Definition**: Discourse indicating participation without engagement, AI-mediated attention allocation, or summarization collapsing polyphony.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| ATTN_SUMM | **Summarization for attention management.** Using AI summaries instead of engaging with full content. Look for: "summarize the meeting," "summary of the document," "AI summary," "condensed version." |
| ATTN_NOTES | **Meeting notes/transcripts.** AI handling meeting documentation. Look for: "meeting notes," "call summary," "transcript," "recap of the meeting." |
| ATTN_CATCH | **"Catch up" via AI.** Using AI to substitute for direct engagement. Look for: "catch up on," "get up to speed," "missed the meeting," "wasn't there but." |
| ATTN_SKIM | **Skimming practice.** AI enabling surface engagement. Look for: "skim," "quick read," "just the highlights," "don't read the whole thing." |
| ATTN_KEY | **Key points/TLDR.** Extracting essentials without full engagement. Look for: "key points," "main takeaways," "TLDR," "bullet points," "highlights." |
| ATTN_CONDENSE | **Condensing communication.** Reducing rich information to simplified form. Look for: "condense," "consolidate," "compress," "boil down." |

**Boundary condition**: Code only when AI mediates attention allocation. Routine summarization of one's own work for others does NOT qualify.

---

### INVEST: Investment Opacity Codes

**Definition**: Discourse indicating concern about effort indistinguishability, credit allocation, skill atrophy, or AI dependence.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| INVEST_SHORT | **Shortcut/cheating discourse.** AI framed as illegitimate ease. Look for: "shortcut," "cheating," "lazy," "easy way out," "taking the easy route." |
| INVEST_APPEAR | **Appearance of low effort.** Worry about appearing lazy. Look for: "look lazy," "seem like I'm cheating," "appear incompetent," "come across as not trying." |
| INVEST_CREDIT | **Credit/recognition concern.** Who deserves credit for AI-assisted work. Look for: "credit," "recognition," "acknowledgment," "attribution," "who gets credit." |
| INVEST_FAIR | **Fairness concern.** Whether AI use is fair advantage. Look for: "fair," "unfair advantage," "level playing field," "fairness." |
| INVEST_SKILL_LOSE | **Skill atrophy concern.** Fear of losing abilities. Look for: "lose my skills," "skills atrophy," "getting worse at," "forgetting how to," "deskilling." |
| INVEST_SKILL_KEEP | **Skill maintenance effort.** Active effort to preserve abilities. Look for: "maintain my skills," "keep my abilities," "still do it myself," "practice without AI." |
| INVEST_DEPEND | **AI dependence concern.** Worry about over-reliance. Look for: "depend on AI," "reliance," "crutch," "can't do without," "dependent." |
| INVEST_COMPETE | **Competition dynamic.** AI use framed competitively. Look for: "compete with colleagues," "keep up with others," "competitive advantage," "others are using it." |

**Boundary condition**: Distinguish INVEST_SHORT (normative concern about legitimacy) from neutral descriptions of time savings.

## PART 2: GOFFMANIAN INTERACTIONAL CODES

These codes capture the dramaturgical dynamics through which opacity mechanisms operate. Apply these codes *in addition to* mechanism codes when relevant.

---

### FOOT: Footing Codes

**Definition**: How participants position themselves relative to AI-generated content in terms of animator/author/principal distinctions.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| FOOT_CLAIM | **Authorship claim.** Asserting ownership despite AI involvement. Look for: "I wrote," "my work," "my words," "I created," "I authored." |
| FOOT_DIST | **Authorship distancing.** Attributing production to AI. Look for: "AI wrote it," "it generated," "the model produced," "AI came up with." |
| FOOT_BLEND | **Blended authorship.** Negotiating joint production. Look for: "we worked together," "I used AI to help," "AI assisted me," "collaborative," "I directed AI." |
| FOOT_AMBIG | **Footing ambiguity.** Uncertainty about authorship attribution. Look for: "not sure who wrote," "hard to say," "blend of me and AI," "whose words are these." |

---

### STAGE: Front/Back Stage Codes

**Definition**: How participants manage audiences and regions in relation to AI use.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| STAGE_FRONT | **Front stage performance.** AI use visible/disclosed to audiences. Look for: "clients see," "openly use," "everyone knows," "transparent about." |
| STAGE_BACK | **Back stage practice.** AI use hidden from audiences. Look for: "privately," "behind the scenes," "they don't know," "secretly," "hidden." |
| STAGE_SEG | **Audience segregation.** Different disclosure to different audiences. Look for: "tell colleagues but not clients," "boss doesn't know," "some people know." |
| STAGE_SHIFT | **Stage shifting.** Movement between front and back stage. Look for: "used to be open, now private," "becoming more public," "changing how I talk about it." |

---

### STIG: Stigma Management Codes

**Definition**: Strategies for managing AI use as potentially discreditable attribute.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| STIG_PASS | **Passing.** Concealing AI use to avoid stigma. Look for: "they don't know I use AI," "pass it off as my own," "hide that AI helped." |
| STIG_COVER | **Covering.** Minimizing visibility of known AI use. Look for: "downplay how much," "don't emphasize," "keep it subtle," "minimize." |
| STIG_DISC | **Strategic disclosure.** Controlled revelation of AI use. Look for: "I tell them I used AI," "transparent about it," "admit I used AI." |
| STIG_NORM | **Normalization effort.** Working to make AI use acceptable. Look for: "everyone does it," "it's normal now," "just a tool," "nothing wrong with it." |
| STIG_BOUND | **Boundary work.** Distinguishing acceptable from unacceptable AI use. Look for: "that's different from," "I don't do what they do," "there's a line." |

---

### LEGIT: Definition of Situation Codes

**Definition**: How participants frame the legitimacy of AI use and negotiate what counts as "real" work.

| Code | Description & Indicators |
|------|--------------------------|
| LEGIT_REAL | **"Real work" framing.** Asserting work is genuine despite AI. Look for: "real work," "genuine effort," "actual contribution," "authentic work." |
| LEGIT_CHEAT | **"Cheating" framing.** AI use characterized as illegitimate. Look for: "cheating," "not fair," "gaming the system," "shortcut." |
| LEGIT_TOOL | **"Just a tool" framing.** Normalizing AI as neutral instrument. Look for: "just a tool," "like any other tool," "same as using," "technology." |
| LEGIT_POLICY | **Institutional framing.** Reference to organizational rules/norms. Look for: "policy," "allowed," "permitted," "company says," "rules about." |
| LEGIT_EVOLVE | **Evolving norms.** Recognition that standards are changing. Look for: "changing," "evolving," "new normal," "didn't used to be," "becoming acceptable." |

## PART 3: CODING INSTRUCTIONS

### Step-by-Step Procedure

1. **First pass**: Read entire transcript without coding to grasp context
2. **Segmentation**: Divide transcript into utterance clusters (thematically coherent segments)
3. **Mechanism coding**: For each cluster, apply all applicable VOICE, VULN, PROV, ATTN, INVEST codes
4. **Interactional coding**: Apply all applicable FOOT, STAGE, STIG, LEGIT codes
5. **Documentation**: Record:
   - Transcript ID
   - Cluster number
   - Verbatim quote (key phrase or sentence)
   - Code(s) applied
   - Brief analytic memo (1-2 sentences explaining coding rationale)
6. **Flagging**: Mark any uncertain cases with [?] for team discussion

### Decision Rules

- **When in doubt, code**: It is easier to remove codes during reconciliation than to add them
- **Multiple codes**: Apply all applicable codes; do not force single-code decisions
- **Context matters**: Consider surrounding utterances when ambiguous
- **Explicit over implicit**: Prioritize explicit statements but code strong implicit signals
- **Boundary cases**: When utterance is borderline, note the ambiguity in memo

### Reliability Protocol

1. **Training set**: All three coders independently code 10 transcripts
2. **Reconciliation**: Meet to discuss disagreements and refine definitions
3. **Reliability check**: Calculate Cohen's kappa on next 20 transcripts
4. **Target**: kappa >= .70 for each code category before proceeding
5. **Ongoing calibration**: Weekly meetings to discuss difficult cases
